PawRides

Our story

At DePauw University, we understand that getting around can be a challenge, especially for students without cars. That's why we founded PawRides, a student-driven, ride-share program aimed at connecting drivers with students who need transportation. Whether it's a trip to a local store, a ride home for the weekend, or a quick trip across town, PawRides is here to make transportation easier and more affordable. Our platform is designed to bring together DePauw students, faculty, and local drivers, offering them a chance to sign up, drive, and help their peers. In return, drivers enjoy benefits like gas reimbursement, exclusive parking perks, discounts from local businesses, and dining hall credits. This initiative not only supports students but also creates a sense of community through shared responsibility and mutual support. With PawRides, we're making travel more convenient, sustainable, and connected for everyone.

Our Founders

Quan Tran

As an international student without a car, Quan faced transportation challenges at DePauw. His own struggles motivated him to co-found PawRides, aiming to provide a practical solution for students in similar situations who lacked reliable transportation options.

Katie Nguyen

Katie encountered transportation issues without a car, which sparked her interest in creating a solution for fellow students. With her background in technology, she co-founded PawRides to offer a convenient, student-centered ride-sharing platform that addresses the needs of the community.

Leonie Nguyen

Leonie, an international student at DePauw, faced challenges when trying to travel both inside and outside Greencastle without a car. Her passion for Computer Science led her to co-found PawRides, aiming to create a convenient, accessible transportation solution for all students on campus.
